# Decision Guide

This guide maps common thinking problems to recommended tools.

The goal is to help users start from the problem, not from the model.

How to use this page:

1. Find the problem that looks most like your situation.
2. Start with the first 2 to 3 tools in that section.
3. Only add more models if the first pass still feels blurry.

## I do not know whether the information is reliable

Use:

- DS-001 Base Rate
- DS-002 Bayesian Updating
- DS-006 Law of Large Numbers
- DS-008 Randomness
- DS-005 Regression to the Mean
- DS-015 Confidence Interval
- DS-016 Confirmation Bias
- DS-027 Survivorship Bias
- DS-097 Signal vs Noise

Core question:

> What evidence should update my belief, and by how much?

Typical situations:

- You saw one news story and want to know if it reflects a real trend.
- A team is overreacting to a recent incident or success.
- You want to judge whether a claim is strong or just vivid.

## I do not know whether the sample is too small or too noisy

Use:

- DS-006 Law of Large Numbers
- DS-008 Randomness
- DS-009 Variance
- DS-015 Confidence Interval
- DS-097 Signal vs Noise

Core question:

> Am I seeing a real pattern, or just normal noise in a small sample?

Typical situations:

- A campaign improved for one week and people want to call it a breakthrough.
- A manager is judging performance from very few observations.
- A new feature looks better, but the sample is still tiny.

## I have too many options and cannot choose

Use:

- DS-007 Expected Utility
- DS-041 Opportunity Cost
- DS-042 Trade-off
- DS-043 Decision Matrix
- DS-044 Decision Tree
- DS-047 Cost-Benefit Analysis
- DS-049 Marginal Thinking
- DS-050 Reversible vs Irreversible Decisions

Core question:

> What am I giving up, and which choice creates the best expected outcome under current constraints?

Typical situations:

- You are choosing between multiple projects, hires, or investments.
- A team has too many good ideas and not enough time.
- A decision feels emotional, but the trade-offs need to be made explicit.

## I do not understand the shape of the risk

Use:

- DS-009 Variance
- DS-010 Distribution Thinking
- DS-011 Black Swan
- DS-012 Fat Tail
- DS-013 Risk vs Uncertainty
- DS-014 Monte Carlo Thinking

Core question:

> Is the real danger in the average case, or in the tail?

Typical situations:

- A plan looks fine on average, but failure would be very costly.
- A team is using optimistic forecasts without looking at downside spread.
- You need to understand whether rare events matter more than normal variation.

## I do not know the real cause of the problem

Use:

- DS-031 First Principles
- DS-032 Five Whys
- DS-033 Root Cause Analysis
- DS-034 MECE
- DS-035 Issue Tree
- DS-036 Constraint Theory
- DS-037 Bottleneck Analysis

Core question:

> What must be true for this problem to exist?

Typical situations:

- The symptom is obvious, but the cause is still unclear.
- A process keeps failing and every fix only treats the surface.
- Different people describe the same problem in conflicting ways.

## I worry about unintended consequences

Use:

- DS-048 Second-order Thinking
- DS-053 Premortem
- DS-055 Scenario Planning
- DS-056 Systems Mapping
- DS-057 Feedback Loop
- DS-061 Delay
- DS-063 Leverage Point

Core question:

> If this works, what happens next? If this fails, why?

Typical situations:

- A policy or feature looks helpful at first glance, but may create side effects.
- You are changing incentives, rules, or resource flows.
- A quick fix may push the problem somewhere else.

## I need to understand a complex system

Use:

- DS-056 Systems Mapping
- DS-057 Feedback Loop
- DS-058 Reinforcing Loop
- DS-059 Balancing Loop
- DS-060 Stocks and Flows
- DS-061 Delay
- DS-062 Emergence
- DS-067 Complex Adaptive Systems

Core question:

> What relationships, feedback loops, and delays are shaping the outcome?

Typical situations:

- The same issue keeps returning after each local fix.
- A system behaves differently than anyone expected.
- Many teams, variables, or time delays are interacting at once.

## I need to make a strategic choice

Use:

- DS-071 Competitive Advantage
- DS-072 Flywheel
- DS-075 Value Chain
- DS-076 Porter's Five Forces
- DS-077 Game Theory
- DS-080 Optionality
- DS-081 Incentives
- DS-090 Principal-Agent Problem

Core question:

> Where is advantage created, protected, or lost?

Typical situations:

- You are evaluating a market, product position, or business model.
- A team is growing, but it is unclear whether the growth is defensible.
- A strategic move looks attractive, but competitor response matters.

## I think people are behaving irrationally

Use:

- DS-021 Loss Aversion
- DS-023 Sunk Cost
- DS-024 Status Quo Bias
- DS-081 Incentives
- DS-084 Social Proof
- DS-085 Mental Accounting
- DS-086 Default Effect
- DS-087 Prospect Theory
- DS-088 Identity Economics

Core question:

> What incentive, fear, identity, or default is shaping behavior?

Typical situations:

- People keep making choices that look irrational from the outside.
- A new rule did not change behavior the way leadership expected.
- A product flow is nudging users more than anyone intended.

## I need better ideas

Use:

- DS-091 Divergent Thinking
- DS-092 Convergent Thinking
- DS-093 Lateral Thinking
- DS-094 Design Thinking
- DS-095 Jobs to Be Done
- DS-098 Inversion

Core question:

> What assumption can be changed, reversed, removed, or reframed?

Typical situations:

- The team needs fresh directions, not just better execution.
- Existing solutions all feel too similar.
- The problem might be framed too narrowly.

## I need to challenge my own thinking

Use:

- DS-016 Confirmation Bias
- DS-096 Circle of Competence
- DS-097 Signal vs Noise
- DS-098 Inversion
- DS-099 Red Team Thinking
- DS-100 Devil's Advocate

Core question:

> What would make my current view wrong?

Typical situations:

- Everyone in the room already agrees, maybe too quickly.
- A high-stakes plan has not been seriously challenged yet.
- You want to test confidence before making a hard commitment.
